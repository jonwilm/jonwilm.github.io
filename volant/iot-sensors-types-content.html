Velocity (speed of motion) sensors may be linear or angular, indicating how fast an object moves along a straight line or how fast it rotates. Acceleration sensors measure change in velocity. Tilt sensors measure tilt on various axes. They are useful in measuring the stability/tilt of platforms, open/close doors, pole lean detection, etc.

Examples:
Accelerometer, TLT, G-Force Snapshot, G-Force Max & AVG, Gyroscopes, Tilt Sensors

Position sensor measures the position of an object referenced either to or from a fixed point or position. They minimize downtime, reduce maintenance requirements, and enhance production efficiency. Uses include measuring variables in offshore oil rigs and monitoring, construction, chemical processing and more.

Examples:
Potentiometer, Inclinometer, Potentiometer

Occupancy sensors detect the presence of people, vehicles and animals in a given area, while the motion sensors detect their movement. Uses are numerous including parking space availability, smart lighting, intruder detection, etc.

Examples:
Ambient Light Sensors, PIR Sensors, RADAR, Electric Eye

Force sensors detect whether a physical force is applied and whether the magnitude of force is beyond a threshold. They are useful in weighing objects and preventing machinery from overloading, monitoring loads on mine lifts, construction cranes, industrial tanks and much more.

Examples:
Tactile Sensor, Force Gauge, Tension Links, Load Pins, Shear Beams

Pressure sensors measure the force applied by liquids or gases. Pressure is measured in terms of force per unit area. They are useful in monitoring pressure-critical equipment such as boilers, water systems, gas turbines, oil drilling systems and more.

Examples:
Barometer, Piezometer, Resonant, Strain-Gauge

Flow sensors detect the instant flow rate of a gas or liquid. They measure the volume (mass flow) or rate (flow velocity) that has passed through a system in a given period of time. Mass flow sensors are widely-used to control the exact dosing of gases or liquids in many technical systems.

Examples:
Turbine Flow Sensor, Electromagnetic Flow Sensor, Thermal Mass Flow Sensor

Level sensors detect the level of substances, including liquids, powders and granular materials. Leakage sensors detect leakage of chemicals, oil and water. It is also possible to detect condensation. Leak detection sensors detect and localize leaks, reducing downtime and inspection time.

Examples:
Hydrostatic, Infrared, Laser, Vapor Sensors

Acoustic sensors measure sound level. Vibration amplitude (the force applied to the object) and frequency (the rate of the back and forth movement) identify predictive maintenance requirements for industrial equipment.

Examples:
Lace Sensor, Geophone, Hydrophone, Seismometer

Humidity sensors detect humidity (amount of water vapor) in the air or other gases. Moisture is a contaminant that exists in industrial gases and the atmosphere. And, it is able to penetrate virtually any surface including such metals as copper, bronze and carbon steel, damaging equipment and wasting energy.

Examples:
Hygrometer, Humistor, Soil Moisture Sensor

Optical sensors monitor any kind of electromagnetic radiation, such as; light, electricity, and a magnetic field.  The molecular recognition elements interact with the target analytes under study and provide information about their presence, concentration, and other physical properties of interest. They are particularly beneficial for remote and multimodal detection.

Examples:
Infrared Sensor, Fiber-Optic, Flame Detector

Radiation sensors detect radiation levels in a given environment. Radiation can be sensed by scintillating or ionization detection. They are useful when monitoring for radioactive contamination as well as inspecting vehicles and freight.

Examples:
Geiger–Mueller Counter, Scintillator, Pyranometer

Temperature sensors measure the amount of heat or cold that is present in a system, detecting physical variations. They can be broadly of two types: contact and non-contact. Contact temperature sensors need to be in physical contact with the object being sensed. Non-contact sensors do not need physical contact, as they measure temperature through convection and radiation.

Examples:
Thermometer, Calorimeter, Infrared Pyrometer

Chemical sensors measure the concentration of chemicals in a system. Gas sensors are used to detect gas leaks or other emissions. Reliable detection of various gases in low concentration is mandatory in fields such as industrial plants, environmental monitoring, or air quality assurance.

Examples:
Infrared Point Sensors, Ultrasonic Sensors, Electrochemical Gas Sensors

Examples:
Rogowski Coil, Hall Effect
